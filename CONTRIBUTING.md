# Contributing Guidelines

Thank you for contributing. This project follows strict structure and discipline to maintain clarity, security, and quality. You are expected to follow the guidelines below.

---

## 🌿 Branch Naming Convention

All branches **must** follow this format:

* `feat: <feature-name>`
* `bugfix: <bug-description>`

### Examples:

* `feat: user-authentication`
* `bugfix: login-token-expiry`

Branches that do not follow this format may be rejected.

---

## 📦 Commit Guidelines

* Keep commits **minimal and meaningful**
* Avoid unnecessary or noisy commits
* Group related changes logically

> Note: This is not strictly enforced, but strongly recommended for maintainability.

---

## 🔀 Pull Request Guidelines

* Prefer **more PRs with smaller, focused changes** rather than large, complex ones
* Clearly describe what your PR does
* Include context and reasoning behind changes
* Ensure your PR follows the provided PR template. For more information, check ```.github/``` for templates for ```PULL_REQUEST.md``` and ```COMMIT_TEMPLATE.md```.

Poorly described or overly large PRs may be rejected.

---

## 🔒 Confidentiality & Responsibility

* Do **not** expose sensitive logic, secrets, or internal details
* Do **not** share or discuss project internals outside authorized scope
* Ensure all contributions comply with the project's proprietary and confidential nature

---

## ✅ Final Notes

* Follow all templates provided in the repository
* Keep code clean, readable, and intentional
* When in doubt, prioritize clarity and security over speed

Failure to follow these guidelines may result in rejection of your contribution.
